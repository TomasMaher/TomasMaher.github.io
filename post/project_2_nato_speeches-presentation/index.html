Project 2: NATO Speeches - Presentation

May 30, 2021

As part of the NATO Speeches Quantitative Text Analysis project I had the opportunity to present my progress mid-way through the semester. Below I outline the PowerPoint slides I used and talk through some changes I would make if I was doing it again.

I used the following title slide to introduce the topic and explain how I was going to approach the presentation

The next slide in the deck introduced the topic of cybersecurity and allowed me to talk about the importance of the topic while also showing how NATO has started to prioritise it. I like this slide but I would likely pick a different colour scheme if I was redoing it.

The next slide was a simple slide highlighting a quote from the secretary general of NATO, Jens Stoltenberg. I used this as a way of highlighting the uncertainty surrounding this serious topic.

Slide 4 introduced my research topic

I used the following slide to highlight my approach. Each of the rows was animated to appear individually so that the audience wouldn’t be overwhelmed. I still like this slide and think it’s a useful way of summarising a lot of information on the process into a concise format.

I tried to summarise some statistics about the dataset on this slide. I think it’s an easy to consume format but in hindsight I would focus more on explaining the context of the speeches (e.g., where were they delivered and why were they delivered)

The following graph shows the most common words in the corpus when stopwords were removed. I used this to highlight the prevalence of the terms defence and Russia within the speeches.

I used the following graph to display my results. I highlighted some real world events that may have affected the prevalence of the topics. This is the slide I would be most inclined to update. I use a locally weighted regression line to display the results but I think a sliding average displayed on a scatterplot is more informative and makes it easier to identify trends. The version I used in the final paper is also displayed below for the sake of comparison .

The following slide was used to display the results of the topic modelling. I used a table format but utilised different colours to highlight the different groupings I identified within the results.

I then took a sample of topics and displayed the results in a table to show how these were labelled. If I was redoing this presentation I would leave this slide out. I don’t think the audience has time to take in enough information from it. I think it would be more useful to display a single topic and explain the methodology using that instead.

Next I displayed the results of the topic analysis in a single graph. I used text boxes to highlight certain trends that helped validate the model. In hindsight, this graph is a bit cluttered and the legend can be confusing. I used a thicker line to highlight the cybersecurity model but the legend makes it look like the cyber topic is blue. I would reduce the number of results displayed in this graph and instead focus on the topics most relevant to the paper.

Finally I concluded by reiterating some of the points made during the presentation and talking about next steps and potential avenues for future research.

I included the references used throughout the slides on a single slide at the end.
